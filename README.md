# Cinema Success Analysis

## Project Overview
This project dives into the world of movies to understand what makes them successful. Using data analysis and visualization, I explored how factors like budget, genre, directors, and audience ratings influence profitability. The idea was simple: can we spot patterns that explain why some films become blockbusters while others flop?

## Objectives
- Look at which genres tend to perform better financially
- See how directors influence consistency and success
- Compare budgets with revenues to test the “big budget = big hit” assumption
- Study audience reception through IMDb scores and reviews

## Dataset
I worked with a movie dataset that includes details like title, year, budget, gross revenue, IMDb score, director, and genre and many more.  
Due to size limits, I haven’t uploaded the CSV here — instead, you can find it on [Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/carolzhangdc/imdb-5000-movie-dataset).

## Key Findings
- Action and adventure films often dominate profitability charts  
- Directors such as Christopher Nolan show consistent success across projects  
- High budgets don’t guarantee high revenue — some mid‑budget films outperform blockbusters  
- IMDb scores generally align with box office performance, but there are exceptions

## Future Scope
